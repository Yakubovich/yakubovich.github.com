Yammer is a social network for internal enterprise collaboration. It's one of the first products to bring the web 2.0 app experience to the enterprise.

As a member of the founding team, I saw the product grow from a concept to a powerful tool used by 200,000 business worldwide, and the team grow from a handful of engineers to 150 employees. The company has been acquired by Microsoft in 2012.

I was in charge of product management, visual design, user experience / interaction design, and some front-end implementation (HTML/CSS/Ruby).


Yammer revolutionized enterprise social networking by bringing consumer-style design and interaction behind the firewall. By using a familiar interfaces inspired by applications like Twitter, Tumblr, and Facebook, we flattened the software's learning curve and allowed users to start socializing right after signing up.

Everyone's related, but we don't always know how.

Geni helps people rediscover those lost connections, and share family stories through collaborative family trees, photos, timelines, maps, etc.

The ultimate goal is to connect the world's family trees, allowing users to see their relationship to any other user, relative, or historical figure. As of 2012, the connected portion of the tree spanned 66 million profiles.

I joined the Geni team as a product manager in 2007 and helped in the conception, design, and implementation of new features like Timelines, Places, Tree Printing, and Tree Merging.

Geni has been acquired by MyHeritage in 2012.





I'm currently the UI designer for commonsku.com.

commonsku is a web-based business management platform powering the promo industry.

In addition to being a complete suite of order management and CRM software, it enables easy collaboration and networking with promotional products professionals, who use it to share best practices, find great products, build closer relationships, and learn from industry experts and peers.

The commonsku home page combines a quick and free sign-up form with a detailed overview of the order management and CRM offering using clean, flat graphics.





The commonsku newsfeed includes order management and CRM activity, as well as industry and internal social networking. The simple and familiar design allows users to get started without wasting time learning a new interface.





Using the social directory, users can quickly connect with others in the promotional products industry through suggestions, and searching by name and company.

I designed the website and online store for RIGHTSLEEVE, a Toronto-based promotional products agency, as well as their summer camp merchandise division, Camp RIGHTSLEEVE.


The RIGHTSLEEVE home page showcases the company's recognizable clients. Unlike typical carousel elements, this design enabling easy and intuitive switching between slides. The thumbnail list smoothly scrolls as slides are switched, allowing for an unlimited number of slides.




RIGHTSLEEVE's camp merchandise division has its own branding and a custom experience running off the same code base. Both sites have a clear and simple call to action, prompting users to contact RIGHTSLEEVE's sales representatives.




Promotional products are displayed using a style similar to that of retail sites, creating an experience that's both familiar and elegant.




Adhering to RIGHTSLEEVE's brand guidelines, the sites uses a clean and simple, magazine inspired layout. Smooth and unobtrusive animations are used throughout the site to create a delightful experience.
